## S2: THE STORY — Chaos to Clarity Illustration

**Filename:** `story-chaos-to-clarity.png`

```
Create an editorial illustration for a solar technology company's website.

Subject: Two halves showing a transformation:
  LEFT HALF (Chaos): Scattered, disconnected icons representing tools that solar project teams use today — a smartphone showing WhatsApp, a spreadsheet grid, a CAD cursor icon, a map pin, an email envelope. These icons overlap chaotically. Thin red dashed lines connect them randomly, showing broken communication. The background has a very subtle warm-gray/reddish tint.
  RIGHT HALF (Clarity): The same icons now arranged in a clean circle around a central hub labeled "pvNXT". Clean teal connection lines flow from the hub to each icon. The background has a subtle cool/lavender tint.
  TRANSITION: A subtle gradient from warm to cool between the two halves. A small white arrow circle in the center marks the transition point.

Style: Editorial line-art — like something you'd see in The Economist or Stripe's blog. Minimal, professional, not cartoonish. Thin lines, subtle fills.

Colors:
  Primary: #069fb1 (teal — for the clarity side and connection lines)
  Chaos side: warm grays, muted red (#ef4444 at 50% opacity) for dashed lines
  Clarity side: cool whites, teal tints
  Background: off-white (#f9fafb) with very subtle grid dots
  Text (pvNXT label): #047f8e (dark teal), bold

Dimensions: 1120 × 1160 pixels (2x for retina)

Purpose: Visually communicate "disconnected manual tools → connected pvNXT ecosystem" without needing words.

Must NOT include: human figures, photos, faces, logos (other than "pvNXT" text in the hub), gradients that feel like a generic AI image, 3D elements, shadows.
```

## S3A: ATLAS PIPELINE — Satellite to 3D Model

**Filename:** `atlas-pipeline.png`

```
Create a technical illustration for a solar SaaS company's website.

Subject: Three connected stages showing how satellite imagery becomes a 3D roof model:

  STAGE 1 — "Capture" (left):
  A stylized satellite in orbit, beaming two offset camera angles toward a rooftop below. The imagery beams are shown as translucent teal cones converging on the roof. Small label below: "Stereo satellite imagery".

  STAGE 2 — "Process" (center):
  A visual showing photogrammetry processing. On the left side of this stage: the two satellite images as small rectangles. From them, thin dotted lines converge into a point cloud visualization (scattered dots forming a roof shape). The point cloud then resolves into a clean 3D wireframe of a roof — a simple house outline with a sloped roof in wireframe style. Small label below: "AI photogrammetry". Tiny note under: "Auto-detects keepouts".

  STAGE 3 — "Output" (right):
  The completed 3D roof model. The wireframe from Stage 2 is now solid — a clean white roof surface. Small rectangular solar panels are placed in neat rows. Dark translucent overlay zones mark shadow areas. Small red markers indicate keepout zones (chimney, vent). Dimension labels on the sides. Small label below: "3D roof model". Tiny note under: "Shadow + BOQ ready".

  CONNECTORS between stages: Thin dashed teal lines with a small glowing teal dot in the center of each connector.

Background: Clean white with very subtle grid pattern (like engineering paper). Light teal tint gradient at the edges.

Style: Technical diagram — clean, precise, minimal. Like an architectural blueprint meets a software UI diagram. Thin consistent line weights. No gradients on the elements themselves — flat, clean.

Colors:
  Primary: #069fb1 (teal — lines, accents)
  Dark: #047f8e (labels)
  Wireframe: #091d2c (dark, thin lines)
  Panels: #069fb1 at 30% fill with teal borders
  Shadows: #091d2c at 20% opacity
  Keepout markers: #ef4444 (red, small dots)

Dimensions: 1200 × 480 pixels

Purpose: Explain the core technology — how pvNXT captures roof data from space without physical site visits.

Must NOT include: human figures, photos, device mockups (no phones/laptops), 3D renders, text other than the labels specified, generic "AI" graphics (no brains, circuits, robots).
```

## S3B: ECOSYSTEM HUB-AND-SPOKE — Connected Products

**Filename:** `ecosystem-hub-spoke.png`

```
Create a hub-and-spoke diagram for a solar SaaS platform website.

Subject: A central hub with 7 product nodes orbiting around it, connected by lines:

  CENTER: A circle labeled "PROJECT DATA" in two lines. The circle has a subtle teal glow. It's the largest element.

  AROUND the center, 7 nodes positioned in a circle (like numbers on a clock face). Each node is a small circle with a teal dot inside, a product name (bold, small), and a one-line function (smaller, lighter). Thin dashed teal lines connect each node to the center.

  THE 7 NODES (clockwise from top):
  12 o'clock: "Atlas" — "Satellite → 3D Model"
  2 o'clock: "Field App" — "Site Verify"
  4 o'clock: "Connect" — "Consumer Portal"
  5 o'clock: "Studio" — "Design & Output"
  6 o'clock: "Proposal" — "Auto-Generate"
  7 o'clock: "Go" — "Track Installation"
  10 o'clock: "SCADA" — "Monitor & Alert"

Style: Clean tech diagram. Minimal. The center circle should have the most visual weight. Nodes are small and subordinate. Connection lines have subtle dash animation potential (but this is a static image).

Background: Transparent or very subtle teal-tinted off-white. The diagram should be usable on both light and dark backgrounds (use white/transparent, not a solid white bg).

Colors:
  Lines: #069fb1 at 30% opacity, dashed
  Center circle fill: #069fb1 at 12% opacity
  Center circle border: #069fb1 at 100%, 2px
  Center text: #047f8e, bold, small
  Node dots: #069fb1 with subtle glow
  Node names: #091d2c, bold
  Node functions: #5d6f7e, light
  Background: transparent (use PNG)

Dimensions: 700 × 700 pixels (square, transparent background)

Purpose: Show that all 7 pvNXT products connect to one shared project data layer.

Must NOT include: 3D effects, gradients on nodes, shadows, any background fill (must be transparent PNG), logos, human figures.
```
